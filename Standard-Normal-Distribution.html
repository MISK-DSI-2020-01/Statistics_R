Zscores are a standardized score that tells us how many standard deviations (i.e.\(\sigma\)) any given value \(x_i\) is away from the mean (i.e.\(\mu\)).

The Standard Normal Distribution

ZScores are described by the Standard Normal Distribution, N(\(\mu\) = 0, \(\sigma\) = 1). Thus an extreme value is outside the interval [-1.96, 1.96] since values outside this range are less than 5% likely to be observed?

ZScores are our first experience with one of the most important equations in all of statistics: signal:noise. Here, signal: \(x_i - \mu\), noise: \(\sigma\).
